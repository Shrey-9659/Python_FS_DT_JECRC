The Project: "The Smart Cafe" Billing & Inventory System

Project Overview
Students will build a terminal-based program where a user can view a menu, place an order for multiple items, see their total bill, and update the cafe's stock.

Key Requirements to Give Your Students
1. The Setup (Data Types & Dictionaries):

Create a dictionary called menu where the keys are item names (strings) and the values are prices (floats/integers).

Create a second dictionary called stock with the same items as keys, and their available quantities (integers) as values.

Initialize a variable total_bill = 0.

2. The Order Loop (Loops & Control Statements):

Use a while loop to keep the program running until the customer decides to checkout.

Inside the loop, display the menu to the customer.

Ask the customer to type the name or number of the item they want to buy.

Validation & Updates (Dictionary Access & Control Statements):

Check Availability: If the item exists in the menu and is in stock, proceed. If it's out of stock or doesn't exist, print a helpful warning message.

Update Data: If available, add the price to total_bill and reduce the item's quantity in the stock dictionary by 1.

Checkout (Exit Strategy):

Provide a specific keyword (like 'checkout') to break out of the loop.

When they check out, print their final total bill.


----- Challenge Steps -----

1. Apply a Discount: If the total_bill goes over $10, apply a 10% discount using a basic math calculation and an if statement.

2. Track Quantities: Instead of ordering 1 item at a time, prompt the user for a quantity, check if the stock has enough units, and deduct that specific amount.
